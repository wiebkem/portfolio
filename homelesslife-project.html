Homeless life

Project
Gamedesign as part of the course Designing User Experiences at Chalmers University

Target user
Gaming people

Timeframe
40 hours within two weeks in 2018

Role
UX Designer

Projectmembers
Independent

Assignment

The design has to evoke a distinct user experience / emotion. This means that it must have a very clear goal regarding which emotion(s) it should evoke. Moreover we had to choose one of the themes whereof I chose the following one: It has to be critical (in the sense that it serves as an eye-opener or a changer of behavior).

Design Process

Before I started with the design, I had an ideation session to come up with an idea.

The ideation session led to the following design goal:
  • Mobile phone game
  • Shows the situation of homeless people
  • It should be a critical design
  • Showing the endless cycle of a homeless person
  • Making it clear to the gamer that it is difficult for a homeless person to climb up the ladder to the ‘normal’ people
  • Invoke the emotion ‘Frustration’
Before I started with the design itself, I conducted a background research to find out how a normal day of a homeless person looks like. Research shows that homeless people are looking for an easy job to get some money, walk around the city to get different benefits in different places, look for a shelter during the night and beg for money. Nevertheless many homeless people are in the situation because they are lazy and don't know how to be responsible or motivated.
After the background research I created a Concept Portrait for the emotion and asked two other people to fill it in for me. This helped me to come up with the different dynamics and mechanics for the MDA framework. The MDA framework is a very helpful tool to figure out the required mechanics within the game to be able to invoke the strived emotion. Based on the MDA framework I created sketches and tried to user test these. It was difficult for users to imagine the game and I continued with wireframes to be able to test the game idea in a better way. These wireframes turned into the prototype which had four iterations in the end.

Final design

Improvements

The game should have more options for places that the gamer can try out. In that case it wouldn't be obvious that the game always ends up in a negative cycle directly. Furthermore, it is important to point out in the beginning which kind of homeless person the gamer is. At the moment this is not clear and can lead to confusion while the gamer is playing the game.
